NCE-02 Lesson 77 A Successful Operation

ordinary version:

A mummy which was dug in Egypt was disappeared from a museum recently. One newspaper reported a unverified but shocking news: the mummy had regain its life and ran away. Many strange stories about the mummies were told one by one everywhere. The public got alarmed by a terrible saying that the mummy would kill children and eat them to recovered.

However, an official announcement was issued from the museum: the mummy was now put in a laboratory where scientists had been studying the mummy to find out the mummy’s history and it would be back in its place soon. The authorized announcement came in time. After learning this, people around the town took a relief and wished the scientists to achieve their goal.

Mistakes Identified:

Original Problem Correction
was dug Missing “up” – “dug up” is the correct phrasal verb for excavating was dug up
was disappeared “Disappear” is intransitive – cannot be used in passive voice disappeared
a unverified “Unverified” starts with a vowel sound → use “an” an unverified
news “News” is uncountable → cannot use “a” before it a piece of news (or delete “a”)
had regain Past perfect requires past participle – “regain” → “regained” had regained
ran away After “had”, need past participle – “run away” run away
the mummies No previous mention of multiple mummies → “the” is wrong mummies (general)
got alarmed “Got” is informal; “became” is better for formal writing became alarmed
saying “Saying” means proverb; “rumor” is more appropriate here rumor
to recovered After “to”, use base verb – “recover” to recover
issued from Standard English uses “issued by” (the issuer) issued by
was now put Tense mismatch – needs present perfect “has been put” has now been put
had been studying Implies studying stopped before the announcement – use “have been” if still ongoing have been studying
the mummy (repeated) Unnecessary repetition – use “it” it
the mummy’s history Better to use “its history” its history
and it would be Missing comma before “and” for clarity , and it will be
took a relief Unnatural expression – use “felt relieved” or “breathed a sigh of relief” felt relieved
wished the scientists to achieve “Wish + someone + to do” is incorrect – use “hoped” or “wished that” hoped that the scientists would achieve

Corrected Version (changes in bold):

A mummy which was dug up in Egypt disappeared from a museum recently. One newspaper reported an unverified but shocking piece of news: the mummy had regained its life and run away. Many strange stories about mummies were told one by one everywhere. The public became alarmed by a terrible rumor that the mummy would kill children and eat them to recover.

However, an official announcement was issued by the museum: the mummy has now been put in a laboratory where scientists have been studying it to find out its history , and it will be back in its place soon. The authorized announcement came in time. After learning this, people around the town felt relieved and hoped that the scientists would achieve their goal.

Additional Minor Improvements (not strictly mistakes, but more natural):

A mummy dug up in Egypt disappeared from a museum recently. One newspaper reported unverified but shocking news: the mummy had regained its life and run away. Many strange stories about mummies were told everywhere. The public was alarmed by a terrible rumor that the mummy would kill children and eat them to recover.

However, an official announcement was issued by the museum: the mummy has now been placed in a laboratory, where scientists have been studying it to find out its history, and it will be back in its place soon. The authorized announcement came in time. After learning this, the townspeople breathed a sigh of relief and hoped the scientists would achieve their goal.
